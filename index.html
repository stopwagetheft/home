Your rights, explained

Tap a topic to learn more — these apply regardless of immigration status.

Every hour you work must be paid at least the state minimum wage — no exceptions for tips, training, or trial periods.

VA $12.77/hr MD $15.00/hr DC $17.50/hr

If you work more than 40 hours in a week, you're generally owed 1.5 times your regular hourly rate for every hour past 40 — even if you're paid a flat salary or "off the books."

It's illegal for your employer to fire, demote, cut your hours, or threaten you for asking about your pay, filing a complaint, or talking to a coworker about wages.

You're entitled to a record of your hours and pay for every pay period. Keeping your own notes — photos of schedules, texts about hours — can help if you ever need to file a claim.

Labor laws protect every worker regardless of immigration status. You have the same right to minimum wage, overtime, and a safe workplace as anyone else.

Beyond the wages you're owed, you may be entitled to additional damages and, in some cases, your attorney's fees covered — at no upfront cost to you.

Statute of limitations: You generally have 3 years to file a claim in Virginia, Maryland, and DC. Don't wait too long.

What Is Wage Theft?

When you don't get paid what you earned

Wage theft happens when an employer fails to pay a worker the full wages they're legally owed. It's the most common form of workplace theft in the country — and most people who experience it don't realize it's illegal, or don't know what to do about it.

Unpaid overtime

Working more than 40 hours a week without getting time-and-a-half for the extra hours.

Paid below minimum wage

Getting an hourly rate lower than what your state requires, even if you agreed to it.

Working off the clock

Being asked to prep, clean up, or keep working after you've clocked out, unpaid.

Stolen tips

Your employer keeping part or all of your tips, or pooling them in ways the law doesn't allow.

Missing final paycheck

Not receiving your last paycheck on time — or at all — after you quit or were let go.

Illegal deductions

Money taken out of your pay for uniforms, breakages, or shortages without your consent.

Common myths

Myth

"I agreed to be paid this way, so it's legal."

Fact

Minimum wage and overtime rules can't be waived, even with your agreement.

Myth

"I'm undocumented, so I have no rights."

Fact

Labor laws protect every worker, regardless of immigration status.

Myth

"I'm paid salary, so overtime doesn't apply to me."

Fact

Many salaried workers still legally qualify for overtime pay.

Myth

"It's too late, this happened months ago."

Fact

You generally have up to 3 years to file a claim in VA, MD, and DC.
